Remember the last time you walked back into an examination room?

Your heart is racing. A knot in your stomach. A place just triggered your trauma : PTSD.

What if we could read that signal — and stop it before it happens?

Why does that happen?

Short answer: the hippocampus.
The long answer: it's a 3-component system. The hippocampus for location, the amygdala as the center for fear, and the amygdala-PAG complex for voluntary behavior and fear response to a threat.

To decode this, we need to record neural activity from this region

Hippocampus
The Setup

A mouse navigates a U-shaped maze.

A mouse runs through a 1 m × 1 m U-maze while we record 20 neurons in the PAG. At a random moment, the mouse is electrically stimulated — triggering a fear response.

Step 1 — Preprocessing

Partition the maze into 3 zones

Curvilinear distance d is just how far along the center path of the U-maze a point is, from 0 (start) to 1 (end).

Simple thresholds on d give us 3 zones — no training needed.

Left — d < 0.35
Top — d < 0.65
Right — d ≥ 0.65
Step 2 — Model

From spikes to a neural representation

Each electrode group's spikes → 1D CNN → 64-dim embedding → Transformer → one vector summarizing the neural state.

Data augmentation: spike dropout (15%) + Gaussian noise

Step 3 — Decoding

Hierarchical prediction with uncertainty

3 parallel heads predict: zone, (x, y) per zone with uncertainty, and d. Final position = softmax-weighted mixture of zone heads.

$$\mathcal{L} \;=\; \mathcal{L}_{\text{cls}} + \mathcal{L}_{\text{pos}} + \mathcal{L}_{d} + 10\,\mathcal{L}_{\text{feas}}$$
$\mathcal{L}_{\text{cls}} = -\sum_c y_c \log \hat{y}_c$ classify correct zone
$\mathcal{L}_{\text{pos}} = \tfrac{1}{2}\log\sigma^2 + \tfrac{(\mathbf{x}-\boldsymbol{\mu})^2}{2\sigma^2}$ (x, y) with learned uncertainty
$\mathcal{L}_{d} = (d - \hat{d})^2$ curvilinear distance
$\mathcal{L}_{\text{feas}} = \text{ReLU}(d_{\min} - w)^2$ stay inside corridor
